### Brand Avoidance
Reject a specific brand, even though it is available, accessible and financially affordable.

**Types:**
1. **Experiential** – Due to bad experience. E.g.: Snap Deal
2. **Identity** – Due to conflict of self-identity and brand's perceived identity. De-individuation – become popular for the masses
3. **Moral** – Conflict between the values of the consumer and those associated with the brand.
4. **Deficit-value** – When they feel that they have not got adequate value in return for the money they pay.
5. **Advertising** – Due to distasteful ad components like content, sound, endorser or frequency.

### Perceived Risk

**Dimensions of perceived risk?**

[Professor's emphasis – red text]:
> Perceived risk is a subjective assessment of what can go right or wrong.
> What are the different dimensions of perceived risk?

There are 6 dimensions to perceived risk:
- **Functional risk** – this laptop is not functioning
- **Physical risk** – this laptop is damaged in delivery
- **Financial risk** – this laptop ate up too much of my money
- **Social risk** – this laptop's speed is embarrassing
- **Psychological risk** – what if this laptop stops suddenly
- **Time risk** – what if the laptop doesn't come to me on time (p188, Kotler and Keller)

[Professor's emphasis – red text]:
> Now, why is perceived risk greater in a credence > experience > search?
> Because the intangibles are higher.
> The risk of using a bad doctor is more damaging than going to the wrong barber.

### [Professor's emphasis – blue text]: What is a Global Brand?

**Define a global brand?**

> Brands whose positioning, advertising strategy, personality, look and feel are in most respects the same from one country to another …are global (Aaker and Joachimstahler, 1999)

> ["Global"] is defined as the multi-market reach of products that are perceived as the same brand worldwide both by consumers and internal constituents (Johansson and Ronkainen, 2005)

> Global brands are those that have global awareness, availability, acceptance, and desirability and are often found under the same name with consistent positioning, image, personality, look, and feel in major markets enabled by standardized and centrally coordinated marketing strategies and programs (Özsomer et al., 2012)

Please read the chapter.
