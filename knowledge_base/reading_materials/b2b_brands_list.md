## B2b brands

Here are some b2b brands worth studying. Remember in b2b brands, the corporate brand and the product brand are often the same. Same is true of many IT and service brands. Other than in consumer products, in all the other categories the product brand is not the prime focus. The corporate brand is. Therefore, studying the companies is inevitable to understand the brand.

> **Note (in red in original):** Besides, the scope of our final exam includes all brands, b2c, b2b, services and IT. You can also be asked to cite a b2b/service/IT example specifically in the question. Which is why I am sending this list. You can use the names given below. But you cannot repeat the details given here. This is just a primer for you. This opens the door to a world we do not normally explore (for some reason branding in the minds of students means just FMCG branding). Read on.

**Astral poly** – is a market leader in CPVC (chlorinated polyvinyl chloride pipes) pipes with 25% market share in plumbing and drainage sectors [https://www.forbesindia.com/article/real-issue/how-astral-pipes-sandeep-engineer-became-a-mintnew-billionaire/52953/1].

**Asian Energy** – a zero debt company, which used to do mining surveys, today diversified into surveying for coal, oil and so on. Future looks interesting.

**NOCIL** - The largest rubber chemical manufacturing company in India. The twists and turns in its history are fascinating. Check out!

**CDSL** – It took 15 years to add 1 crore demat customers. The next 1 crore got added in a year. The next one crore got added in 2 months. Imagine, as India gets more and more financialized and formalized how big it can get.

**Indiamart** – there are 6.5 crore MSMEs in India. Of these only 20000 to 30000 are on an online platform. Eventually even if 20% of all the MSMEs get on to this platform think how strong this brand can get. (But is this threatened with the entry of JDMart of Reliance? Not many customers are switching between us and Udaan [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z3-WNzqZqbQ, Deep Dive Into Indiamart Intermesh & Swotlight On Glenmark Life Sciences | Inside Out | CNBC-TV18, 1,083 views, 2 Apr 2022, 9:30 to 11:30 am])

**Ion Exchange** (water purification) – has been performing very well for the last 4 years, check out why?

**MTAR** – a company that does cutting edge work for Nuclear Fuel Complex, BARC, ISRO. Got listed recently.

**Teamlease** – it is a company that helps in staffing, recruiting etc. Its founder Manish Sabharwal's views are often sought for understanding employment problems in India. He and CMIE are 2 sources that tell us how the job market works in India. (One must see video of his for all MBA students is "Why is India Poor? Manish Sabharwal talks at Manthan")

**Fiem industries** – they are in auto lighting, they are helping auto companies move to LED lighting, that is a big market, small but interesting company, reducing its debt, great traction in future.

**Lumax** – 60% share in automobile lighting in India

**Gabriel springs** – leading shock absorber and front fork manufacturers for 2 wheeler and 4 wheelers. It has 25 percent market share in 2W segment, 65 percent in 3Ws

**Sona Koyo** – a Deming Prize winner for quality that makes automobile steerings. The other two well known names that won the coveted quality award are Sundaram Clayton and Sundaram Fasteners.

**Blue Dart** – well-run company, steadily reducing debt, charges a premium compared to competitors (check how they do it), unlike smaller companies managed Covid well through minimal touch delivery.

**Endurance technologies** – country's biggest aluminum casting company.

**Essel Propack** – Global leader that supplies soft squeeze tubes (e.g. your toothpaste tube) and a leading supplier to P&G and Unilever.

**ISGC Heavy** – initially into sugar plants and now diversified into several sectors (iron castings, presses, boilers, air pollution equipment) and countries. Interesting company.

**Motherson Sumi** – biggest wire harness manufacturer in India for cars, headwinds are chip shortage for car companies hurting it and slowing its harness consumption, raw material prices have gone up in the recent past.

**Garware technical fibre** – global leader in fishing net fibre manufacturer

**Divis Labs, Dr Reddy Labs, Lupin Labs, Cipla, Ranbaxy, Sun pharma** – all worth studying. Cipla is particularly good in "nasal" and has a better India footprint than competitors.
