Countries and cities are competing world-wide not only for tourists but also for export markets, business investment and skilled migrants. To meet tourism, business and population objectives, destination managers are now applying branding principles, derived from product/goods marketing, to establish their destinations as brands. While branding has been applied to consumer products for decades, the concept of strategically branding countries, cities and regions really only appeared during the mid-90s to assist destinations to compete more effectively in an increasingly competitive marketing environment. Generally it is perceived that destination branding happens in two phases- the first phase is to promote the place for tourism and following this, the second phase is to promote the place for business. For instance, Incredible India is the B2C brand promoting India as a tourism destination where as India Inc. is the B2B brand attracting foreign direct investments.

Branding provides a way of creating a unique identity through relationship building and emotional appeal along differentiation on the basis of functional qualities. This study aims to understand the various dimensions involved in destination branding in order to construct a comprehensive tool for measuring and creating brands.

---

[Page 2] *(pp. 95 in original)*

The movement from commodity to branding is complex in the case of products which do not fall in "goods category". Branding of Services in general and Branding of places in particular requires conscious effort and overcoming several challenges. In destination branding scenario, this confusion is created due to the presence of tangible and intangible elements. Most of the literature in branding per se refers more to goods and services. On the other hand, with the inevitable discourse of globalization and prospering tourism industry, branding of destination is an emerging area of research. With the spurting growth of new tourist destinations all around the world and the competition for reaching out to travelers, a creation of a unique identity is essential for any destination. Through this exploratory research, an attempt is made to capture the abstract dimensions in destination branding.

Place branding involves much more than the establishment of a clever tag line and logo. The brand tools must be based on a clear articulation of the brand essence reflecting both the attributes of the destination and its people and the benefits sought by consumers. Without the umbrella of an overarching strategy for the destination brand experience, tactics such as the aforementioned may garner short-term gains in building awareness, but may lack the staying power vital to the long-term good health and well-being of a brand.

In this era of super-brands it may seem an unusual notion to consider a city, state, region or country as a brand. We buy brands because we enjoy them and they make our choices easier. In the context of a place being the focal point for the promotion and catalyst for economic development, it makes sense that it should be managed as a brand to enhance its identity and perceived value in an increasingly competitive marketplace.

## DESTINATION BRANDING

Destination branding is a process to create a unique identity for a particular destination to give a unique feel to the consumers or visitors about the destination. Destination branding is all about to identify the distinct element of a particular destination and communicating a message to retain and attract the visitors of the place. According to Jayswal (2008) "A destination is a town, city or a place which has one or more attractions for tourists". These may be in the form of scenic sights, culture, leisure activities, shopping rebates, food and excursion, etc.

---

[Page 3] *(pp. 96 in original)*

Destination branding helps to increase the image of a particular destination which in turn helps promote tourism, trade development, foreign investment and bilateral agreement and many more features. Moreover the places which have gone through major political and economic changes can be re-branded. Destination branding normally occurs in two phases: In the first phase the destination is promoted as a tourist spot so that the customer gets a feel of the place. Tourism is normally built around historical legacy, culture, natural landscape and assets, developed infrastructure and facilities. Following this the second phase works on promoting it for events, conferences, trade and foreign investments, etc.

Tourism promotion is the essential part of the destination branding. Visitors are attracted to a destination because of the popularity of the destination and promotion helps to get the popularity of the destination. Every destination has positive and negative aspects including the civil wars, corruption, and lack of infrastructures, etc. Therefore sometime it's difficult for the tourism department to promote the destination, but promotion can be done by assuring tourist that these factors are not going to affect them by showing the security and support of the destination to tourists.

Branding of a destination is based on some major components like brand image, brand culture, brand essence, brand personality, brand character and brand identity. Brand image is related with the visitors' perception about the destination. Brand culture includes the cultural aspects, local people, language and other features of the destination. Brand essence is the emotional appealing of the destination. Brand personality includes the exciting, realistic message of the destination. Brand character includes the honesty, integrity of the destination marketer. Brand identity is the way to create the perception about the destination for visitors by the destination marketers.

Destination branding is not a simple task; selecting the verbal and visual elements for the brand logo and tag line to use is one of the difficult tasks for destination marketing organization (DMOs). These verbal and visual elements of a destination brand should represent the values and identity of a destination and capture the perceptions of its visitors. Normally DMOs encourage their partners to become consistent with the

---

[Page 4] *(pp. 97 in original)*

visitors' conception including the beauty, peace, historical monuments, food etc because these are the part of the destination brand.

Branding of a destination can be done by pairing with other objects like place, events, monuments, great people etc. This co-branding has a direct impact to the host destination. Moreover the successful co-branding helps to promote the destination among tourists whereas failure of the co-branding leads to the negative image about the destination.

## CONCEPT OF SEVEN SISTERS

From ancient times, India's Northeast region has been the meeting place of many communities, faiths and cultures. It is a place of magical beauty and bewildering diversity. There are more than 166 ethnic tribes speaking many languages. Some groups have migrated over the centuries from places as far as Southeast Asia. They retain their cultural traditions but are beginning to adapt to contemporary lifestyles. Northeast is unique in lushness of its landscape, the range of communities and geographical and ecological diversity. The region creates enchanting and romantic atmosphere in all the seasons. The Northeast region includes eight states of Sikkim, Arunachal Pradesh, Assam, Meghalaya, Tripura, Mizoram, Manipur and Nagaland but they are popularly referred to as seven sisters.

The concept of tourism development is very recent in this region and is in its infancy. They have taken a few strides in developing tourism, with emphasis on eco-tourism, wildlife tourism, cultural tourism and adventure tourism. The region has tremendous and diverse potential for tourism development, which could attract international and national tourists. However, the tourism development of the northeast region needs to be targeted via a regional approach rather than an individual state approach. Selling the N-E states as the best destination for tourism will require futuristic vision, conscious and planned efforts at all levels and a disciplined action plan and implementation strategy. In the meanwhile, sympathetic approach of the central government will be required to stabilize the socio-political situation in the northeast followed by overall infrastructure development in the region.

## LITERATURE REVIEW

A destination is a place in which a customer or visitor stays temporarily. Visitors are allowed to visit only authorized places included in the geographical boundary of the destinations. Tourism departments provide

---

[Page 5] *(pp. 98 in original)*

all the basic support to the visitors. A particular destination visits are dependent on the culture, weather and climate, ancient places, security and promotion of the tourism. Marketing of a destination is essential to attract the visitors as well as to retain the popularity of the places. Global competition helps tourism industry to create the destinations with unique characteristics as destination brands.

Branding of a destination is continuously increasing day by day. Hankinson (2001) found that destination branding involves many challenges relating to the branding of a place by destination marketers. The factors that affect the destination branding are organizational complexity and control, the management of partnerships, product complexity and the measurement of success. Wilkins et al., (2008) in their study found that the destination should be comfortable, safe, friendly and relaxing with good accommodation to establish it as a destination brand.

Baker and Cameron (2008) identified in his research that critical success factors (CSF) are essential for destination branding and categorize the CSF as Strategic orientation, Destination identity and image, Stakeholder involvement, and Implementation, monitoring and review.

Konecnik and Go (2008) identified the importance of the brand identity concept on destination branding in their study. Olins (2002) found that, sometimes countries change their names and countries have always been engaged in building and transforming their identities to become more visible. Creation of the identity convey brand message to the visitors and it links to the destination with visitors. For instance, Paris is known for romance, Rio for fun, Milan for style and Dubai for shopping.

Hankinson (2007) suggested a theoretical framework for the management of place brands with the idea of external and internal brand identity of a destination that the DMO manages and described the distinct features of complex destination product in six categories: the co-production of the place product, the co-consumption of the place product, the variability of the place product, the legal definition of boundaries, administrative overlap and the political accountability.

Morgan et al., (2002) indicated in their research that destination brand is a collaborative process and the creation and the management of a destination brand are described as requiring collaborative effort among stakeholders. Aas et al., (2005) found that collaboration among the stakeholders is essential and it should be balanced and properly maintained

---

[Page 6] *(pp. 99 in original)*

among them because everyone has different role and benefit from the tourism.

Laws et al. (2002) indicated in their model collaborative destination and operator marketing that, every operator have different segment of customers and they have to attract them through different appeal and advertising under the destination authorities. Destination branding needs the cooperation of destination operators and authorities, to create the image and brand through the cooperative marketing.

Organizing an event in a place helps to create a better image for the host destination and it creates a co-brand for both the event and place. Co-branding has positive and negative impact because failure of the event will decrease the image of host place (Xing and Chalip, 2006).

Freire (2009) found that local people play a crucial role in place or destination branding because local are going to interact with the visitors. Moreover knowledge of English language and commercial dealings of local people with visitors plays a role on brand building. Hence it can be said the image of a destination is depend on the local people of the place.

Gnoth (2002) indicated that the development of a place as a destination brand creates the advantage for the products and services of that destination and the diversified community groups of the same attract the tourists and increase the tourists experience by creating diverse image of a destination. Kotler & Gertner (2002) identifies that the country image has an impact on the product and services of a country and the image can increase the reputation of a country by positive or negative way.

Tourist destination image is a wide and global feeling about the destination. Images of countries should be properly projected to the world because destination images influence a tourist's travel decision-making, cognition and behaviour at a destination as well as satisfaction levels and recollection of the experience (Jenkins, 1999). Ooi (2004) claimed that the destination branding promotes the brand message of that particular place.

Papadopoulos and Heslop (2002) suggested that destination branding of a country requires the effective branding strategy to position the country in the mind of visitors and failures would lead to decrease the image of that country. Caldwell and Freire (2004) have suggested that country and other destinations should be branded differently because countries are

---

[Page 7] *(pp. 100 in original)*

functionally diverse whereas regions and cities, being smaller in scale and therefore the branding strategies for all three types of tourist destinations should be different.

The Anholt Nation Brands Index is the first analytical ranking of the world's destination brands, and represents the people's perception of destinations including tourism and represents various factors on destination brand evaluation. According to Konecnik (2004) the process of evaluating the image of a destination can be seen as a self-analysis process towards building a brand. Due to the competition between the destination's image becomes a critical element and therefore self analysis process is essential to build the strong destination brand.

Therkelsen (2003) indicated about glocal strategy (combined local and global approaches) and how it influence the image formation processes of potential tourists culture specific and shared elements. Glocal approach helps the tourism destination in a way that the images are recognizable world-wide leading potentially to a consistent and strong image.

From the previous studies taken up, what emerges is that destination branding helps the visitors to find the suitable and secure destination for their vacation or leisure purpose. Visitors want the destination atmosphere to be safe, secure and friendly. A destination is promoted by the destination marketers or tourism department of the destination collaborative with other stake holders of the destination. It has been seen that destination branding has a limitation because it only focuses on tourism ignoring the other stakeholders of the destination. Normally a destination is promoted by the destination marketing organizations, but research indicated that different destination operator is working under destination authorities to promote the destination. These destination operators have different approach to attract visitors, different appeal and advertising and they are targeting different segment of the visitors' to the destination.

One of the major aspects of destination branding is that the same strategy of destination branding cannot be used for different destination branding purpose. Every destination has different cultures, languages, living standard and geographic locations – destinations have diverse criteria and this require the different strategies to build the brand. Moreover in a particular destination there are different characters like in a country having diverse places where the same strategy cannot be used. Research shows that the country based marketing is not working properly because

---

[Page 8] *(pp. 101 in original)*

of inadequate understanding by visitors about the meaning of country branding.

Brand identity concept on destination branding is an important feature. It depends on the brand identity as to how the destination will be perceived by the visitors. Destination branding sometimes became more important for a place because if that place has gone through major changes in terms of political or economical scenario, then the place has to re-brand to attract the visitors. Therefore brand identity creation for the place is essential to convey the message and promote the place among the visitors. Literature review shows that the destination branding increases the consumption of products and services of a place and creates the image of a destination among visitors. Research also shows that the images of destination have direct impact on visitors travel decision. Image of a destination is related with the local people of that destination because local people are going to interact with the tourists commercially or socially to create the experience for visitors.

Previous researches also indicated the importance of the constant evaluation of the image of destination because of the increasing competition between destinations to attract the tourists. Image of destination is based on the beliefs, ideas or expectation that people have about the place. Since image is the result of the identity communicated, marketers have to carefully analyze consumer perceptions to propose a destination brand identity. This research paper discuses a tool designed for mapping consumer perception and mindsets (brand image) which will be used by different stakeholders to propose a brand identity.

## RESEARCH METHOD

The research method used for the study was a mixture of case study and Delphi method. The focus of the case study was the north eastern states of India, also known as the seven sisters of India. The perception about and the attitude of travelers towards northeast states had to be studied in depth for creation of a tool which will support in branding this entity as the seven sisters of India. The literature related to the north eastern states was collected from the view point of a traveler. The case study of north eastern states helped in discovering the aspects and the related attributes necessary for construction of a quantitative tool. This was followed by a five stage Delphi method. In the first stage all the issues related to the branding exercise was laid down and discussed. The unit of study for this research was debated in the second stage. Based on the

---

[Page 9] *(pp. 102 in original)*

modifications with respect to the second stage, the filtering of needed information was discussed in the third stage. The fourth stage involved the ordering of questions in the tool for data collection (Questionnaire). In the final stage, the validity and the reliability of the questionnaire were checked and the respective additions and omissions were carried out.

## DEVELOPMENT OF FRAMEWORK

Destination branding can be quite complex given the number and nature of stakeholders involved. Brand India in general stands for management of anarchy in the best possible way. This tells us something essential about Brand India. It is a brand of contradictions, of immeasurable complexity and unsurpassed diversity. Seven Sisters is only one segment of Brand India. What we need, therefore, is some kind of framework—a set of tools, some shared points of reference and points of view—to analyze, discuss and understand the Destination Brand phenomenon. It is important to remember that branding is as concerned with the real world as it is with perceptions. If attempts to brand are too far from the truth, they become spin or empty marketing, meaningless slogans or logos. At best these are a waste of money, and at worst they create unrealistic expectations and cynicism. Intentions can be good, but when self-perception and the outside perception of what is realistic don't match, we can create gaps that become easy targets.

The Questionnaire was designed to map the consumer mindsets and perceptions related to seven sisters of India. The questions framed the following model created by the authors.

Questions covered related to brand awareness, associations, personality, culture, quality, category, activity and price. These components collectively help measure Brand knowledge which in turn lets the Brand image of a place emerge. Brand image can be used by marketers and stakeholders to propose the brand identity. The brand identity thus created hence is more aligned to brand image which can thus lead to effective marketing.

---

[Page 16] *(pp. 109 in original)*

### Fig. 1: Destination Branding Framework

The framework is a two-level model diagram. Eight boxes appear at the top level, each connected by a downward-pointing arrow into a single central box labelled "Brand Knowledge" at the middle level. From "Brand Knowledge", one downward arrow leads to a box labelled "Brand Image" at the bottom level.

The eight input components feeding into Brand Knowledge are:
1. **Brand Awareness**
2. **Brand Associations**
3. **Brand Category**
4. **Brand Quality**
5. **Brand Activity**
6. **Brand Price**
7. **Brand Culture**
8. **Brand Personality**

*Interpretation: All eight dimensions of consumer knowledge about a destination collectively constitute Brand Knowledge. Brand Knowledge then shapes Brand Image. Brand image, once measured, can be used by destination marketers and stakeholders to propose a Brand Identity that is grounded in consumer reality rather than wishful projection.*

## CONCLUSION

The data collected by means of this questionnaire will provide information which can be further processed as knowledge for meaningful application. The set of demographic and psychographic scales constructed were original. The tool can be easily administered in a very professional way. Utmost care has been taken to avoid loaded, leading and double barreled questions which usually are ignored by many researchers when they undertake exploratory research. The design of the questionnaire which is compact in four A4 size pages does not create discomfort for the respondent.

The questionnaire was designed around creating a match between reality and image. Consumer mindsets were captured through Brand Awareness, Brand Knowledge, Brand Quality, Brand Category, Brand Culture, Brand Offerings, Brand Activity, Brand Price, Brand Personality.

A mixture of ordinal, internal and ratio scales have been used to measure the perception and attitude of tourist destination. The battery of statements and concepts were constructed to capture all variables related to the study. Moreover this would enhance the utilization of efficient statistical tools and techniques for researchers to bring out meaningful interpretations. Thus the data gathered through this instrument would

Keeping the generic format intact, the questionnaire with selected modifications can be effectively employed for other destination branding studies. The validity and reliability of the instrument should be examined before other studies are undertaken.
