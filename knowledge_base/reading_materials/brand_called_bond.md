## A brand called Bond

"My name is Bond, James Bond". Almost every person who speaks English knows these lines. Few franchises last as long as James Bond. Bond movies are famed for their action, guns and girls. Bond kills the bad guys, plays doubles or quits in shooting and gambling, romances women and keeps the British Union Jack up. He ends up collecting 7 billion dollars over 25 movies. Critics and movie goers, men and women, producers and distributors love the franchise. But what holds the key to the James Bond franchise? Does the character espouse a consistent set of values? Has it been true to its core? What explains the fascination for this fictional character?

### The Beginning

Ian Lancaster Fleming, a British journalist, born in 1908, etched the character of James Bond in his books. Fleming also served in World War 2 as assistant to the Director of Naval Intelligence in the Admiralty in London. Post retirement, Fleming started his work on James Bond. The first Bond novel, Casino Royale, was published in 1953 and followed by 13 more (Fleming died in 1964). Post Fleming's death, Bond novels were continued by writer John Gardner from 1981 to 1996 (14 books, 2 novelisations) and Raymond Benson from 1996 to 2002 (6 books, 3 novelisations). The original character of Bond, as Fleming intended is slim, athletic, with a scar down his right cheek, not prone to use disguises, speaks French and German, smokes heavily and has 2 vices - alcohol and women.[^1]

### The Franchise

The first James Bond movie, Dr. No, was released on May 8th, 1962. More than half a century of Bond movies has seen a total of 25 films and 6 actors grossing $7.06 billion worldwide,[^2] third highest amongst all franchises, only behind Marvel Cinematic Universe and Harry Potter.

The Bond novels have also been adapted on television (as special features, documentaries and cartoon series), radio and comic strips. Eon Productions Limited (based in United Kingdom) and Danjaq LLC, the holding company of Eon (based at United States of America), wholly owned and controlled by Broccoli and Wilson families, own the copyright to produce James Bond movies.

Since the first movie in 1962 the Bond franchise has grown in strength and numbers. More people watch it today than ever before due to its growing familiarity and availability in local languages. Half of a century of his presence made Bond almost a household name in most parts of the world. But has Bond remained the same or has he changed? Are these changes incremental or fundamental? Has the central theme of Bond's character been preserved or has its DNA changed?

### Question

Take Goldfinger as the standard. Note i) core values ii) supporters iii) detractors iv) peripherals v) absentees of brand Bond as you infer them from Goldfinger. See how far the brand has changed between Goldfinger and Skyfall based on values i) thro' v). Note that a snippet might stand for more than one value. Based on your observations take a view on whether the brand Bond has changed a) fundamentally b) significantly c) incrementally. Also comment on whether these changes might help or erode the brand's equity.*

### Movie synopsis – Goldfinger

Auric Goldfinger is a multimillionaire. He has a devious plan to multiply his millions. He owns a lot of gold already. Now he wants to blow up Fort Knox (the place where the United States Government holds it in safekeeping) to multiply the value of the gold he has. James Bond thwarts his plans.

### Characters in Goldfinger

M – James Bond's boss, Q – supplier of hi-tech gadgets to Bond, Goldfinger – villain, Oddjob – villain's henchman
